Weather Preparedness

Be Prepared. Stay Safe.

"Weather can change quickly, but being prepared makes all the difference."

First Warn Weather doesn't just tell you what's coming — we help you understand what to do about it. From knowing your shelter locations to having a plan before severe weather arrives, preparedness is the mission.

📱 Stay Connected

Follow First Warn Weather on Facebook and TikTok for real-time updates during active weather events.

🏠 Know Your Shelter

Identify the lowest, most interior room of your home or building before severe weather season begins.

⚡ Have a Plan

Discuss your severe weather plan with your family before an event — knowing what to do in advance saves lives.

🎒 Build a Kit

Water, food, flashlight, batteries, medications, and important documents — ready before you need them.

🌊 Flood Safety

Never drive through flooded roads. Turn around, don't drown — just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.

❄️ Winter Ready

Keep an emergency kit in your vehicle during winter months — blanket, boots, jumper cables, and sand or kitty litter.

About the Meteorologist

Meet Dr. Nick

Meteorologist PhD · Higher Education Leadership Executive Juris Doctorate Public Safety Professional Author Ordained Minister

Dr. Nick is a meteorologist and public safety professional with years of experience communicating weather information clearly and accurately to the communities he serves.


Holding a PhD in Higher Education Leadership and an Executive Juris Doctorate, Dr. Nick brings both scientific expertise and a deep commitment to community education to every forecast. His goal is simple: give you the information you need, in a way you can actually use it.


When he's not forecasting, Dr. Nick writes for Dr. Nick's Matter of Perspective, serves as an ordained minister, and spends time with his four Corgis — Willow, Mogwai, Winston, and Baxter.
