Submit a Storm Report

📍 Your Report

✓ Report received! Thank you for helping keep the community safe.

For life-threatening emergencies always call 911 first. This form is for weather observation reporting only.

📋 What Makes a Great Report

📍
Be Specific About LocationInclude cross streets, highway mile markers, or nearby landmarks. "Near Hwy 61 and Elm St in O'Fallon" is far more useful than just "St. Charles County."
🌪️
Tornado vs. Funnel CloudA tornado touches the ground. A funnel cloud is aloft. Note which you observed and the direction it was moving.
⚡
Hail Size MattersCompare to common objects — pea, dime, quarter, golf ball, baseball. Pick up a piece to measure if safe to do so.
🌊
Flooding DepthEstimate water depth over roads using landmarks — "water halfway up stop sign post" or "covering front porch steps."
🌬️
Wind DamageNote what was damaged — tree limbs, fences, power lines, roofs. Estimate the diameter of fallen trees if possible.
🔒
Safety First AlwaysNever put yourself in danger to make a report. Shelter in place during a tornado warning. Report after the storm passes if needed.

Join the Network

Apply to Be a Weather Watcher

We're always looking for dedicated, safety-conscious individuals to join the First Warn Weather Watcher network. No prior experience required — just a passion for weather and your community.

✅ Who We're Looking For

Residents of our Missouri or Illinois coverage counties who can observe and report weather conditions reliably. NWS SKYWARN training is a plus but not required — we'll point you to free training resources.

📋 What Watchers Do

Submit storm reports before, during, and after significant weather events. Provide Dr. Nick with on-the-ground observations to verify radar data and NWS information. You report — we'll handle broadcasting.

🎓 Training & Resources

Free NWS SKYWARN training is available online at weather.gov/lsx/skywarn. Completion of SKYWARN Basic earns you a certification badge on your watcher profile.

📸 Your Profile on FirstWarnWX

Active watchers get a featured profile card on this page — including your photo, bio, county coverage, certifications, and specialties. Your contribution is recognized publicly.
